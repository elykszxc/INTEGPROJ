Childhood Years Back to Top

       I was born and raised in Manila, and growing up as a child was great. 1 My earliest memories are filled with carefree days spent playing outside with friends, and only worrying about what games to play, and who to play it with. 2 Although my family wasn't whole, I was still surrounded by love and encouragement, which shaped my personality. 3 My parents always emphasized the importance of education and hard work, instilling in me values that I carry to this day. 4 I spent most of my time outside, playing basketball, and games like patintero and habulan. 5 Sometimes my friends and I stayed up late just talking to each other with each of our own stories. 6 School was an exciting adventure, and I eagerly absorbed knowledge, excelling in subjects that piqued my curiosity. 7 I remember the first time I joined a major competition, a spelling bee, however I only got 2nd place. But it still made a big impact in my life, because I was actually good at it, which made learning enjoyable. 8 My childhood was also marked by small yet significant lessons in resilience and determination, whether it was overcoming fears, making new friends, or dealing with challenges. 9 Weekends were often spent at my dad's side, and weekdays at my mom's side, where both gave me a strong appreciation for family. 10 Summers were the highlight of my childhood, because as a child, no classes were the best. 11 I had a strong imagination, often creating stories and games that made every day feel like an adventure. 12Looking back, I cherish the innocence and curiosity that defined those early years. 13 The friendships I made during this time, though some faded over time, played a vital role in shaping my social skills. 14 I also remember moments of failure, whether it was losing that spelling bee or quiz bee, or struggling with a difficult lesson, but they only pushed me to try harder. 15 My childhood wasn’t without challenges, but those experiences taught me patience, perseverance, and the ability to adapt. 16 I was always eager to learn new things, whether it was how to ride a bike, or build a school project from scratch. 17 My parents often shared stories about their struggles and successes, inspiring me to dream big. 18 Holidays were filled with laughter, family gatherings, and cherished traditions. 19 My curiosity led me to explore different hobbies, from basketball to playing the guitar, shaping my interests. 20 Though I didn't know it then, the experiences of my childhood laid the foundation for the person I would become.
